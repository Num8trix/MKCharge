EV charger FAQs

Everything you need to know about EV charger installation

Straight answers on cost, grants, timelines and what to expect — from local OZEV-registered engineers who install across Milton Keynes every week.

Cost & grants

A standard home EV charger install in Milton Keynes typically costs between £750 and £1,200 depending on your consumer unit, cable run length, and whether groundwork or extra electrics are needed. We give you an exact, fixed price after a free survey — no vague "from" prices that change on the day.

Homeowners are generally no longer covered by the old OZEV home grant, but flat owners, renters, and landlords can often still claim under the EV Chargepoint Grant, and many workplaces qualify for the Workplace Charging Scheme. We check your eligibility for free and handle the paperwork if you qualify.

Yes, but charging overnight on a cheap EV tariff is almost always far cheaper per mile than petrol or public rapid chargers. Use the savings calculator on our homepage for a rough estimate based on your weekly mileage.

Booking & timelines

Most Milton Keynes installs are surveyed, approved with your DNO (UK Power Networks locally), and fitted within about a week. The install itself usually takes 2–4 hours on the day, with no long-term disruption to your driveway.

An engineer visits to check your consumer unit, the distance from it to your parking space, and your supply capacity. You'll get a fixed quote and a confirmed install date on the spot — usually within a few days of getting in touch.

Property & eligibility

It's possible in some Milton Keynes streets using a cable channel or council-approved gully, but options are more limited than off-street driveways. We'll assess your specific street during the free survey and talk you through what's realistically achievable.

Leaseholders and renters usually need written landlord consent before installation. Most driveway installs in Milton Keynes don't need council planning permission, though listed buildings or conservation areas can be the exception — we'll flag this during your survey if it applies.

Yes — we install multi-unit charging setups for offices, retail sites and fleets across Milton Keynes, and can advise whether your business qualifies for the Workplace Charging Scheme.

Choosing your charger

Tethered chargers have a built-in cable, which most drivers find more convenient day-to-day. Untethered (socket-only) units look neater and let you swap cables if you ever change car brands. We'll recommend the right fit based on your car and how you park.

A 7kW charger roughly halves charging time compared to 3.6kW, and is the standard choice for most single-phase homes. Which one you can have depends on your electrical supply — we confirm this during the survey.
