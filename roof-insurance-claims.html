Roof insurance claims in Milton Keynes

Updated 2026 · 6 min read

Storm damage and fallen branches are among the few roofing problems that home insurance typically does cover — but making a successful claim usually depends on acting quickly and having the right evidence. Here's what's generally covered, what isn't, and how to put together a strong claim.

Quick answer: sudden storm or impact damage is usually covered; gradual wear, lack of maintenance, and pre-existing issues generally aren't. Always check your specific policy wording, and get a roofer's report as soon as possible after the damage occurs.

What's typically covered

  • Storm damage — tiles blown off or cracked by high winds, usually covered as "sudden and unforeseen" damage.
  • Fallen branches or trees — impact damage from a tree or branch coming down on the roof.
  • Escape of water following storm damage — resulting internal damage from water ingress after a storm event.

What's usually not covered

  • Gradual wear and tear — ageing tiles, worn flashing, or moss build-up that deteriorates over years.
  • Lack of maintenance — damage insurers consider preventable through reasonable upkeep.
  • Pre-existing damage — anything that was already present before the storm event you're claiming for.

This is a general guide — always check your own policy's specific terms, as coverage varies between insurers.

How to build a strong claim

  1. Act quickly. Contact a roofer to stop further water ingress as soon as it's safe to do so — most insurers expect you to prevent further damage.
  2. Photograph everything. Both the external damage and any internal water ingress, ideally dated.
  3. Get a roofer's report. A short written assessment describing the cause and extent of damage supports your claim considerably.
  4. Keep receipts for any emergency temporary repairs — these are often reimbursable as part of the claim.
  5. Contact your insurer promptly and follow their specific claims process alongside the above.

Common questions

Will making a claim increase my premium?

It can, depending on your insurer and claims history — worth weighing up against the cost of repair for smaller jobs, particularly if you're near your excess amount.

Do I need multiple quotes for an insurance claim?

Some insurers ask for this, others use their own approved contractors. Check with your insurer early in the process to avoid duplicate work.

How long do I have to make a claim?

This varies by insurer and policy — check your documentation, but in general it's best to notify your insurer as soon as possible after the damage occurs.
