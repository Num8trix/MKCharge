GUTTERING & FASCIAS

Guttering & fascia repair in Milton Keynes

Updated 2026 · 6 min read

Overflowing gutters and sagging fascias are one of the most common (and most ignored) roofing problems in Milton Keynes. Left alone, they don't just look bad — trapped water can work its way into the roof structure and cause far more expensive damage than the gutter repair itself.

Quick answer: a straightforward gutter clear and repair typically costs £100–£300; full gutter, fascia and soffit replacement on a semi-detached house runs roughly £900–£2,200 depending on length and material. Get an exact price with a free quote.

Signs your gutters or fascias need attention

  • Water overflowing during rain — usually a blockage from leaves and moss rather than a structural fault.
  • Gutters pulling away from the fascia board — often means the fascia behind it has started to rot.
  • Staining or damp patches on external walls — a classic sign water is missing the downpipe entirely.
  • Visibly sagging or misaligned guttering — brackets failing or wood swelling behind the gutter run.
  • Peeling paint or soft, spongy fascia boards — a sign moisture has already got into the timber.

What causes the damage

Blocked gutters are the root cause of most fascia problems. Leaves, moss and general debris build up, water backs up instead of draining away, and that trapped water sits against the fascia board — usually timber or uPVC-faced timber — until it starts to rot or warp. Left long enough, that rot can spread into the roof's timber structure underneath the tiles.

How to avoid repeat problems

  • Have gutters cleared at least once a year, more often if you're near mature trees
  • Fit gutter guards if leaf-fall is a recurring issue on your street
  • Ask your roofer to check brackets and joints while they're up there for any other job
  • Consider uPVC fascias over painted timber if you're replacing anyway — less ongoing maintenance

Common questions

Can I clear my own gutters?

You can for single-storey, easily accessible sections, but for anything above ground-floor height it's safer and usually cheaper overall to have it done properly with the right ladder or tower setup.

Will my insurance cover fascia rot?

Generally not — rot from lack of maintenance is treated as wear and tear rather than sudden damage, so it's rarely covered. Storm-related guttering damage is a different matter.

How long do uPVC fascias last?

Typically 20–30 years with minimal maintenance, compared with painted timber which usually needs repainting every few years and can rot sooner if not kept on top of.
