Chimney repair & repointing in Milton Keynes

Updated 2026 · 6 min read

Chimneys take more weather punishment than almost any other part of the roof — they're the highest point, fully exposed on all sides, and often decades older than the roof covering around them. Crumbling mortar joints (repointing) and cracked or leaning stacks are two of the most common reasons a chimney needs attention in Milton Keynes.

Quick answer: repointing a chimney stack typically costs £350–£700; a full rebuild of the stack above roofline runs roughly £1,200–£2,500 depending on height and access. Get an exact price with a free quote.

Signs your chimney needs attention

  • Crumbling or missing mortar between bricks — the clearest sign repointing is overdue.
  • A visibly leaning stack — can indicate the mortar has failed enough to affect structural stability.
  • Damp patches on chimney breast walls inside the house — often flashing or mortar failure letting water in.
  • Loose or fallen pieces of masonry around the base of the chimney — a sign of active deterioration, and a safety hazard.
  • Cracked or slipping chimney pots — can let water track down inside the flue.

What causes chimney deterioration

Mortar is more porous than brick, so it's usually the first thing to fail under constant exposure to rain, frost and wind at height. Once mortar joints start crumbling, water gets further into the brickwork, and freeze-thaw cycles in winter accelerate the damage. Flashing where the chimney meets the roof is a separate common failure point, often confused with chimney problems themselves.

Reducing future chimney problems

  • Have a visual chimney check every few years, especially on stacks over 30–40 years old
  • Fit a chimney pot cowl or cap if the flue isn't in regular use, to keep rain out
  • Ask your roofer to check flashing at the same time as any general roof work
  • Don't ignore small mortar cracks — repointing early is far cheaper than a rebuild later

Common questions

Is a leaning chimney dangerous?

It can be, particularly in high winds. If you notice a visible lean, it's worth getting it assessed promptly rather than waiting.

Do I need scaffolding for chimney work?

Often yes, especially for a full rebuild — chimney work at height needs secure access, and a roofer will factor this into the quote.

How long does repointing last?

Good quality repointing typically lasts 20–30 years before it needs attention again, depending on exposure.
